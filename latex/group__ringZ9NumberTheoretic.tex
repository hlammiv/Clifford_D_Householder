Computes Galois conjugate of given instance. 

The Galois group of $ \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_9) $ is the cyclic group of order six. The Galois group consists of field automorphisms from $ \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_9) $. By \textquotesingle{}automorphism,\textquotesingle{} we mean abort bijective function form the field to itself which a) distributes over addition, multiplication, subtraction, and division, and b) maps 0 and 1 to themselves. Because of these properties, any Galois automorphism is defined uniquely by where it maps $ \zeta_9 $ (which must also get mapped to a root of unity). In particular, the Galois automorphisms in this case exponentiate the root of unity in each term to some power $ k $, where gcd(k,9) = 1. That is, each term $ a_i \zeta_9^i $ becomes $ a_i \zeta_9^{i*k} $.

If $ \mbox{sde}_\chi $ of instance is less than 6, this computes it using Theorem 3.\+6 of the Kalra paper. 

Theorem 3.\+6 in Kalra et. al. states that an element of this ring is divisible by $ \chi^k $ for $ k < 6 $ iff the augmentation (see \doxylink{group__ringZ9NumberTheoretic_gadf42b1a70cc5a8d0452631461023c56d}{ring\+Z9\+::signed\+Weight}) of the $ (k-1)$-\/th formal derivative divided by $(k-1)!$ is divisible by 3. Our formal derivatives are calculated using \doxylink{group__ringZ9NumberTheoretic_ga90e0f7652b46a17e218094f1c639f91a}{formal\+Derivative}.

The reason I created this is because Theorem 4.\+3 in the Kalra paper says that the sdes in terms of $ \chi $ in any entries in a unit vector (and therefore, also a unitary matrix) will be the same. This means, when looking through candidates for the diagonal and x\+\_\+3 in ESA, we can disregard any combination whose sde\textquotesingle{}s disagree.

\begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
With our usage, we can assume this member function will only be called on ring elements whose $ \mbox{sde}_\chi $ is less than 6 (since this is equivalent to the ring element being indivisible by 3). Thus, if this function is used on an element divisible by 3, this function returns 6, even if the true sde is higher. ~\newline

\end{DoxyNote}
\begin{DoxyReturn}{Returns}
the sde in terms of $ \chi $ if less than 6, and 6 otherwise. 
\end{DoxyReturn}
